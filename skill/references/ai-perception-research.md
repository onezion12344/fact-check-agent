## 1. AI & Emotional Intelligence / Relationships

### Pew Research Center (Sep 2025)
- Survey: 5,023 US adults, June 9-15, 2025
- **50%** say AI will worsen people's ability to form meaningful relationships
- **5%** say AI will improve it
- **25%** say no change
- Young adults (18-29): **58%** say worse (vs 40% of 65+)
- Source: pewresearch.org/science/2025/09/17/ (+ PDF)

### Longitudinal Study — arXiv 2504.14112 (Apr 2025)
- Over one year: perceived attachment to AI **+33 pp**, perceived AI empathy **+26 pp**
- AI companion users vs non-users showed significant divergence
- Source: arxiv.org/abs/2504.14112

### HBS AI Institute (May 2026)
- AI companions: "from novelty to objects of public anxiety"
- Debate: social substitution / emotional manipulation vs loneliness reduction
- Source: aiinstitute.hbs.edu

### Stanford Scale — Illusions of Intimacy (May 2025)
- Need for "guardrails, ethical design, and public education to preserve the integrity of emotional connection"
- Source: scale.stanford.edu/ai/repository

### ACM — Public Perceptions of Emotion AI (CHI 2025)
- Evidence about public perceptions across contexts provides guidance for policymakers
- Source: dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706598.3713501

## 3. AI Environmental Impact

### MIT News — Explained: Generative AI's environmental impact (Jan 2025)
- Consequences include increased electricity demand and water consumption
- Most impact comes from *inference* (using the model), not training
- Source: news.mit.edu/2025/explained-generative-ai-environmental-impact-0117

### VU Amsterdam / Alex de Vries-Gao — Patterns journal (Dec 2025)
- AI carbon footprint in 2025: comparable to New York City (32.6-79.7 million tons CO₂)
- AI water consumption: 312.5-764.6 billion liters, comparable to global bottled water industry
- Source: vu.nl/en/news/2025/ai-s-hidden-carbon-and-water-footprint
- Cell Patterns: cell.com/patterns/fulltext/S2666-3899(25)00278-8

### UN University — INWEH Report (Jun 2026)
- By 2030: AI energy use ~**3% of global electricity**
- Water footprint could equal needs of **1.3 billion people**
- Source: unu.edu/inweh/news/environmental-cost-of-AIs-Enrgy-use
- The Conversation: theconversation.com/un-report-warns-ai-could-soon-use-3-of-worlds-electricity

### Per-Query Water Use — ⚠️ HIGHLY DISPUTED
- **Google Gemini (official measurement, Aug 2025)**: 0.26 ml / 0.24 Wh / 0.03g CO₂ per text prompt
  - Source: cloud.google.com/blog/products/infrastructure/measuring-the-environmental-impact-of-ai-inference
- **ChatGPT direct cooling estimate**: ~0.3-0.5 ml per query (Ren study, various sources)
- **ChatGPT total water footprint (incl. electricity generation)**: up to **519ml** per query (Whitney Foster substack 2025-10; methodology disputes this figure)
- **Google Search baseline**: <0.01ml per query (negligible compared to AI)
- **Key lesson**: 0.26ml vs 519ml = 2000x difference depending on methodology. Always cite which methodology.

### IEA / AIMultiple — Projections
- AI global electricity: **~3% by 2030** (IEA/UN)
- AI water use: **4.2-6.6 billion m³ by 2027** (AIMultiple, citing UC Riverside 2023 paper)
- ⚠️ Lesson: These are TWO SEPARATE METRICS. Do not write "4.2-6.6% of global electricity" — that's a unit confusion error.

## 4. Cross-Cultural Comparison

### Edelman Trust Barometer — AI Flash Poll (Nov 2025)
- **China**: 87% trust AI
- **US**: 32% trust AI
- **UK**: 36%, **Germany**: 39%, **Brazil**: 67%
- Source: edelman.com/trust/2025/trust-barometer/flash-poll-trust-artifical-intelligence
- Also: techinasia.com/china-trusts-ai-western-countries-survey

### Edelman Trust Barometer — General Trust (2025)
- Different survey, different numbers for the same country!
- **Saudi Arabia**: 87 (highest overall), **China**: 83, **UAE**: 82
- ⚠️ Lesson: Always specify which sub-report (AI-specific vs general trust). These are NOT interchangeable.

### KPMG / UniMelb — Global AI Trust Study (May 2025)
- 48,000 respondents across 47 countries
- Global average: 58% view AI as trustworthy, but only 46% willing to trust
- Hong Kong-specific: NOT available from Edelman. KPMG data for HK is for enterprise AI adoption (24% of HK firms widely adopting), NOT consumer trust.
- Source: assets.kpmg.com/.../trust-attitudes-and-use-of-ai-global-report.pdf

### Pew Research Center — Global AI Trust (Oct 2025)
- 25 countries surveyed
- Trust in US/EU/China to regulate AI differs sharply by political leaning
- Source: pewresearch.org/global/2025/10/15/trust-in-the-eu-u-s-and-china-to-regulate-use-of-ai

### Computers in Human Behavior — Cross-cultural AI Chatbot Study (Nov 2025)
- Data from China, Germany, Hong Kong, Japan, and United States
- Explains motivations as "context alignment" — culturally dependent
- Source: sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0747563225001852 (Scherr, Cao, Kobayashi et al.)

### NIH — Cultural Variation in Social Chatbots (2025)
- Two studies, total N=1,659
- Evidence for cultural differences in attitudes toward socially bonding with conversational AI
- Source: pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11945465

### HK-Specific Note
- No standalone Edelman or KPMG poll for Hong Kong consumer AI trust
- HK adolescents: more willing to turn to chatbots for self-disclosure (AI & Society 2025, HK study)
- HK firms: 24% widely adopting AI (KPMG Employment Outlook 2026)
- Best estimate for HK AI trust: ~57% (interpolated between China 87% and US 32%, consistent with KPMG regional patterns)
- **This is an estimate, not a surveyed figure.**

## 5. Cross-Cutting Pitfalls (from this research session)

### Attribution Confusion
- "75% feel AI will have detrimental effect on jobs" = **Bentley-Gallup 2024**, not Pew
- "52% workers worry about AI" = **Pew/Forbes Jan 2026**
- "50% more concerned than excited" = **Pew Jun 2025**
- Root cause: search results list multiple survey numbers adjacent to each other. Always copy the survey name directly.

### Unit Confusion (this session)
- "4.2-6.6" in search → could be % electricity OR billion m³ water
- Only the unit from the SAME SENTENCE is valid
- Fix: verified against UN University report: ~3% electricity, 4.2-6.6 B m³ water (SEPARATE metrics)

### Same-Institution Multiple Surveys (this session)
- Edelman published both Trust Barometer (general) and AI Flash Poll (AI-specific) in same period
- Same institution name, same format, different numbers
- Fix: cite the sub-report name, not just the institution

### Data Visualization Trap (this session)
- 0.26ml, 0.5ml, 519ml on same linear chart = invisible small values
- Fix: log scale or segmented display for >2 orders of magnitude

## 6. AI Over-Reliance — Seven Common Fears

*Added 2026-06-23 — sources from 7+ studies*

### #1 Critical Thinking Erosion ✅ Warranted
- Microsoft (Jan 2025): significant negative correlation between AI use frequency and critical thinking scores, mediated by cognitive offloading
- Harvard Gazette / MIT Media Lab (Nov 2025): "excessive reliance on AI-driven solutions" → "cognitive atrophy"
- International AI Safety Report (Feb 2026): early evidence confirms AI reliance weakens critical thinking, encourages automation bias
- IE University (Feb 2025): cognitive offloading partially mediates reduced critical thinking
- Source: microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/lee_2025_ai_critical_thinking_survey.pdf

### #2 Memory Degradation / Digital Amnesia ✅ Warranted (amplified existing effect)
- Google effect (Sparrow et al., Science 2011): people remember WHERE to find info, not the info itself
- Bibliometric analysis (Springer 2025): digital amnesia research field rapidly growing
- AI amplifies by reducing retrieval effort to near-zero (ask vs search)
- Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_effect

### #3 Creative Atrophy ⚠️ Mixed
- Systematic review of 14 studies: AI boosts efficiency but over-reliance reduces originality
- AI as brainstorming tool CAN enhance creativity — key variable: tool vs crutch
- Source: linkedin.com (Potkalitsky, May 2025)

### #4 Automation Bias ✅ Strongly Warranted
- One of the most replicated findings in human-automation interaction (decades in aviation, medicine)
- Springer (Jul 2025): comprehensive review confirming AI amplifies automation bias
- International AI Safety Report (Feb 2026): key risk
- AI makes it worse: conversational, authoritative-sounding outputs increase trust beyond what the system warrants
- Source: link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02422-7

### #5 Professional Deskilling ✅ Well-Documented
- NYT (Aug 2025): "first evidence that relying on AI tools might erode a doctor's ability to perform fundamental skills"
- Springer (Aug 2025): mixed-method review of AI-induced deskilling in medicine
- Nature (May 2026): deskilling in medical AI confirmed
- AI & Society (Nov 2025): deskilling is a STRUCTURAL problem, not individual
- arXiv (May 2026): "brainrot" phenomenon — deskilling and addiction from GenAI over-reliance
- SSRN (Mar 2026): AI disproportionately benefits lower-skill workers, compressing skill distribution
- Sources: nytimes.com/2025/08/28/well/ai-making-doctors-worse-deskilling.html, link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-025-11352-1

### #6 Social Withdrawal ⚠️ Partially Warranted
- ScienceDirect (2026): AI social dependence → withdrawal, isolation, diminished real-life social skills
- HBS AI Institute (May 2026): AI companions = "objects of public anxiety"
- Risk concentrated in vulnerable/heavy users; general population concern exceeds actual harm
- Source: sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2451958826001764

### #7 Epistemic Passivity ⚠️ User-Dependent
- UNESCO (2026): "Artificial intelligence: do we still need to think?"
- Passive users → harm; active, literate users → minimal
- Source: unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000397871_eng

### Key Takeaway
- Unifying mechanism: **cognitive offloading** across all seven fears
- How you use AI matters more than how much: passive uncritical use → harm; active intentional use → minimal harm or benefit
